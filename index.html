Microbots: how do they work?

Micro-robots capable of swimming like micro-organisms hold tremendous potential for biomedical applications such as drug delivery and micro-surgery. However, swimming under the microscope poses significant challenges due to the stringent constraints imposed by fluid dynamics at small scales. Common swimming strategies used at larger scales become largely ineffective. In recent decades, scientists and engineers across different disciplines have come together to develop a range of micro-swimmers capable of navigating the microscopic world.

To enhance public understanding, we have created an educational game that allows players to gain virtual hands-on experience with the exciting applications of micro-swimmers in an entertaining manner. The game was developed by an interdisciplinary team of students from Santa Clara University, including students from mechanical engineering, computer science, and digital arts. Our goal is to provide an enjoyable experience while showcasing the possibilities offered by micro-swimmers. If you have any questions about this outreach effort, please feel free to email us at opak@scu.edu. We hope you enjoy playing the game!

The microswimmer in the game is based on the pioneering work by Najafi and Golestanian (Physical Review E, 69, 062901, 2004). Support by the National Science Foundation (Grant numbers 1830958 and 1931292) is gratefully acknowledged.


How to Play:

1) Press "1" and "2" on your keyboard to control the microbot

2) Find a way to move the bot forward

3) Break the blood clot to win
